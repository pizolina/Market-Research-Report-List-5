<p><ul><li>Ionic</li><li>Nonionic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the wool-protective agents market, there are two main types: ionic and nonionic. Ionic agents contain charged particles that can attract and bond with dirt and stains on the wool fibers. Nonionic agents, on the other hand, do not have a charge and work by repelling dirt and preventing it from sticking to the wool fibers. Both types of agents are used to protect wool items from damage, but they achieve this through different mechanisms of action.</p></p>
