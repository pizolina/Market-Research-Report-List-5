<p><p>Direct current meters are used to measure the flow of electrical current in a single direction, primarily in applications such as solar panels and battery charging. Alternating current meters, on the other hand, are designed to measure the flow of electrical current that constantly changes direction, commonly found in household and industrial electrical systems. Three-phase smart meters combine both types of meters to accurately measure energy consumption in three-phase electrical systems, providing utilities and consumers with real-time data for efficient energy management.</p></p>
