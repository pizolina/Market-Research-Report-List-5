<p><p>Medical contrast agents are substances used to enhance the visibility of tissues and organs during medical imaging procedures. There are four main types of contrast agents: Barium-based contrast media, which are used for imaging the gastrointestinal tract; Iodinated contrast media, commonly used in X-rays and CT scans; Gadolinium-based contrast media, primarily used in MRI scans; and Microbubble contrast media for ultrasound imaging. Each type has specific properties and applications, but they all aim to improve the clarity and accuracy of medical imaging results.</p></p>
