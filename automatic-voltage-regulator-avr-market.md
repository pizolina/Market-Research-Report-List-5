<p><p>Automatic Voltage Regulators (AVR) are used to regulate and stabilize voltage in electrical systems. The market for AVR can be segmented into three types: linear regulators, shunt voltage regulators, and others. Linear regulators provide a constant output voltage by dissipating excess power as heat. Shunt voltage regulators maintain a constant output voltage by diverting excess current away from the load. The "others" category includes various types of voltage regulators such as switching regulators, IC voltage regulators, and programmable voltage regulators.</p></p>
