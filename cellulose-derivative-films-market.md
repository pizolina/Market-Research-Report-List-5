<p><p>Cellulose derivative films can be categorized into two main types: coated films and uncoated films. Coated films are cellulose films that have been treated with a coating material to enhance their properties such as moisture resistance, glossiness, or printability. On the other hand, uncoated films are cellulose films that have not been treated with any additional coating material. Both types of films are widely used in packaging, pharmaceuticals, food products, and other industries for their biodegradability, sustainability, and barrier properties.</p></p>
