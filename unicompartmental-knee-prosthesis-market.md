<p><p>Unicompartmental knee prosthesis market types include cemented bone and non-cemented bone options. Cemented bone prostheses involve the use of bone cement to secure the implant in place, providing immediate fixation. On the other hand, non-cemented bone prostheses rely on the natural bone's ability to grow into the implant for long-term stability. Each type offers distinct advantages and considerations for patients based on factors such as bone quality, age, and activity level. Both options aim to improve knee function and alleviate pain for individuals with knee joint degeneration.</p></p>
