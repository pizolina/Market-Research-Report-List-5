<p><p>Secondary switchgears are essential components in electrical systems and are available in various types such as gas-insulated and vacuum-insulated. Gas-insulated switchgears use sulfur hexafluoride gas to provide insulation and are commonly used in high-voltage applications due to their compact design and high reliability. On the other hand, vacuum-insulated switchgears use vacuum interrupters to extinguish the arc and offer better protection against electrical faults. Both types of switchgears play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and efficiency of electrical distribution systems.</p></p>
