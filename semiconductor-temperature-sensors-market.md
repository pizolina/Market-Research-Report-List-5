<p><p>Semiconductor temperature sensors comprise various types such as Voltage Output Temperature Sensors, Current Output Temperature Sensors, Digital Output Temperature Sensors, Resistance Output Silicon Temperature Sensors, and Diode Temperature Sensors. Voltage output sensors provide temperature data in the form of voltage levels, while current output sensors give temperature readings through current signals. Digital output sensors offer temperature readings in digital format, and resistance output Silicon temperature sensors provide temperature data based on changes in resistance. Diode temperature sensors rely on the forward voltage drop across a diode to measure temperature.</p></p>
