<p><p>External defibrillators are medical devices used to restore normal heart rhythm in someone experiencing cardiac arrest. There are two main types in the market: Automated External Defibrillators (AED) and Manual External Defibrillators. AEDs are designed for use by non-medical personnel and provide audio and visual prompts to guide users through the process. Manual defibrillators, on the other hand, require trained medical professionals to deliver a shock at the right time. Both types play a crucial role in saving lives during cardiac emergencies.</p></p>
