<p><p>Bedside monitors are classified into three categories based on the level of care they are designed for. High-acuity monitors are intended for critical care patients in intensive care units, providing detailed real-time data on vital signs and organ functions. Mid-acuity monitors are for patients in step-down units or general wards, offering continuous monitoring of key parameters. Low-acuity monitors are used for patients with stable conditions in non-critical settings, providing basic monitoring of vital signs. Each type of monitor caters to different levels of patient acuity and care needs.</p></p>
