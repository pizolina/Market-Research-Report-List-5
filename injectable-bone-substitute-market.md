<p><strong>The Injectable Bone Substitute Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Injectable bone substitutes are materials used to fill bone defects or voids in order to promote bone growth and regeneration. These substitutes can be categorized into two main types: natural and synthetic. </p><p>Natural injectable bone substitutes are derived from natural sources such as coral, ceramics, and demineralized bone matrix. They have the advantage of being biocompatible and promoting bone growth, but may have limited availability and pose a risk of disease transmission.</p><p>Synthetic injectable bone substitutes are man-made materials such as calcium phosphates, bioactive glasses, and polymers. They are designed to mimic the properties of natural bone and provide structural support. While synthetic substitutes may lack the biological activity of natural materials, they offer more consistent quality and production control.</p></p>
