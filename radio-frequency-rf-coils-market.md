<p><p>RF coils are used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) systems to generate and receive radio frequency signals. Volume coils are larger and used for whole-body imaging, while surface coils are smaller and used for more focused imaging of specific body parts. Volume coils are more commonly used in clinical settings due to their versatility, while surface coils are used for higher resolution images in research and specialized imaging applications. Both types of coils play a crucial role in the functioning of MRI systems.</p></p>
