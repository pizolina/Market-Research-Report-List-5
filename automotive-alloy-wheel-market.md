<p><p>Automotive alloy wheels come in various types, including casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold to create the wheel shape, resulting in a cost-effective option suitable for most vehicles. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping the wheel by applying pressure and heat, resulting in a stronger and more durable option preferred for high-performance vehicles. Other types in the market may include hybrid or composite wheels, offering unique features and designs to cater to specific needs or preferences.</p></p>
