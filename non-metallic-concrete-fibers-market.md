<p><p>Macro-synthetic fibers are large-sized fibers typically made from materials such as polypropylene and polyester. They are commonly used in concrete applications to improve durability, crack resistance, and impact strength. On the other hand, micro-synthetic fibers are much smaller in size and are also made from materials like polypropylene and nylon. They are used in concrete to enhance properties such as plastic shrinkage crack control and freeze-thaw durability. Both types of fibers are important in the non-metallic concrete fibers market for providing reinforcement and improving the overall performance of concrete.</p></p>
