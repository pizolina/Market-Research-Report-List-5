<p><p>Home audio crossovers are devices that split an audio signal into different frequency ranges before being sent to individual speakers. There are two main types of markets for home audio crossovers: active and passive. Active crossovers require external power to operate and are typically used in high-end audio systems for their flexibility and precision. Passive crossovers, on the other hand, do not require external power and are more commonly used in budget-friendly audio systems for their simplicity and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
