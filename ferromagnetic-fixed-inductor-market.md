<p><p>Ferromagnetic fixed inductors are commonly categorized into two main types: multi-layer type and wire wound type. Multi-layer inductors are constructed by layering multiple conductive material plates and insulating layers, providing higher inductance values in a compact size. Wire wound inductors, on the other hand, are made by winding a conductive wire around a core material, allowing for higher current handling capabilities and lower resistance. Both types are widely used in electronic circuits for filtering, energy storage, and signal processing applications.</p></p>
