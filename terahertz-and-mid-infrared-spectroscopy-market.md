<p><p>Terahertz spectroscopy involves the study of materials using radiation in the terahertz frequency range, offering insights into molecular structures. Mid-infrared spectroscopy focuses on wavelengths longer than near-infrared but shorter than far-infrared, providing information on molecular vibrations. Near-infrared spectroscopy examines shorter wavelengths primarily for analytical purposes. Mid-infrared spectroscopy is widely used in areas such as pharmaceuticals and chemicals, while terahertz spectroscopy is gaining popularity in fields like security screening and medical imaging. Far-infrared spectroscopy, with even longer wavelengths, is utilized for studying lattice vibrations in solids.</p></p>
